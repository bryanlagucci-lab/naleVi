Role One

Writer & Blogger

Collaborate →

Words are Leah's first instrument. Long before titles accumulated and disciplines multiplied, she was writing — observing the world around her and finding language for what she saw.

As a writer and blogger, she covers the terrain where life gets interesting: East African tourism and travel, fashion and personal style, gender and social issues, and the everyday complexity of professional life in Uganda. Her writing is characterised by an honest, exploratory voice — never preachy, always inviting the reader to think alongside her.

She has contributed to Ugandan and pan-African digital publications, maintained her own blog, and produced long-form and short-form content for brands, nonprofits, and advocacy campaigns. She understands that great writing doesn't just inform — it changes the way people see things.

Role Three

Life & Business Coach

Book a Session →

This is where Leah's impact is most felt and most often spoken about. Coaching is not what she does on the side — it is at the centre of how she understands her purpose in the world.

Her coaching style defies easy categorisation. It is structured without being rigid. It is empathetic without being soft. It is challenging without being harsh. She has an extraordinary ability to see what a client is actually saying beneath what they are telling her — and to ask the single question that unlocks a breakthrough.

Whether working with an entrepreneur who has lost momentum, an executive navigating a leadership transition, or a young professional stepping into their first role of real responsibility — Leah meets them exactly where they are and walks with them until they are ready to run on their own.

Over 200 individuals across Uganda and beyond have experienced her coaching. Many return. Many send others. That is the truest measure of what she delivers.

Role Four

Fashion Enthusiast & Stylist

Book a Styling →

Fashion, for Leah, is never superficial. It is one of the most direct languages human beings have — a way of communicating identity, intention, and confidence before a single word is spoken.

As a stylist and fashion advocate, Leah works with clients — particularly professional women — to discover and articulate their personal style in a way that feels authentic rather than forced. She doesn't impose trends; she uncovers the version of the client that is already there, waiting to be expressed with confidence.

Her styling philosophy is deeply rooted in African fashion culture. She celebrates the richness of Ugandan and East African textile traditions — Ankara prints, Kitenge, and contemporary African design — alongside professional and international styling conventions. The result is a style language that is globally fluent and unapologetically African.

Beyond individual clients, Leah uses her platform as a fashion enthusiast and influencer to champion body positivity, cultural pride in fashion, and the idea that every woman — regardless of budget, size, or background — deserves to feel extraordinary in how she presents herself.

Role Five

Team Leader & Advocate

Engage Leah →

Leadership, for Leah, is not a position — it is a practice. And advocacy is not optional — it is a moral imperative. Both flow naturally from who she is and what she believes.

She has led teams across diverse sectors — from office environments to community organisations to nonprofit programmes. What distinguishes her leadership is the combination of decisive authority and genuine care. She holds high standards while creating the psychological safety that allows people to meet them. Teams under her leadership don't just perform — they grow.

Her advocacy work is equally defining. She speaks and acts on behalf of gender-inclusive opportunities in professional spaces, championing the idea that talent, ambition, and capability have no gender. She engages with humanitarian causes, social policy, and community development — not as a bystander, but as an active, committed participant.

She has represented organisations at forums and summits, facilitated community dialogues, and contributed to programmes that have created measurable change in the lives of Ugandan women and families.

Her Leadership Principles

Lead by Example

Speak the Truth

Leave No One Behind

Success shared with the team is success multiplied. She lifts as she climbs.

Role Six

Nonprofit & Governance

Partner with Leah →

Impact without structure doesn't scale. Leah brings the discipline of professional administration and strategic thinking to the world of nonprofit management and governance — where the stakes are highest and the resources are often thinnest.

She has worked alongside and within community organisations, NGOs, and social enterprises — contributing to their strategic planning, operational management, fundraising frameworks, and board governance. She understands the unique pressures of the nonprofit sector: the need to demonstrate impact, manage donor relationships, and maintain accountability while staying true to the organisation's social mission.

Her approach to nonprofit work is never purely administrative. She brings genuine social conscience and a commitment to lasting, measurable impact. She asks the hard questions: Is this programme actually changing lives? Are we measuring the right things? How do we sustain this beyond the next grant cycle?

That combination of professional rigor and human-centred values makes her an asset to any organisation committed to doing good — and doing it well.
